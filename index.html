Scary Shawarma Kiosk — Unofficial Wiki

Scary Shawarma Kiosk is a Roblox horror-management game where you run a late-night shawarma stand while identifying and handling anomalies. Success depends on serving real customers, rejecting threats at the right time, and passing inspections. The community is large and active, and mechanics evolve with updates, so this page focuses on clear, practical guidance.

Gameplay

Balance speed and accuracy: prepare orders correctly for normal customers, and reject anomalies by closing the shutters when required. Mistakes raise your error count. At 2:00 AM on Fridays an inspection event occurs with specific escape handling. Many anomalies are timing-based, meaning when you act is just as important as what you do.

Core loop

Serve normal customers fast and clean; identify anomalies and react with the correct action (serve, reject, or follow unique steps).

Reject means shutters

When a guide says “reject,” close the kiosk shutters. Some events require closing then reopening as part of the script.

Error management

Serving an anomaly or rejecting a safe customer adds errors. Five errors causes the inspector to "fix you" A.K.A killing you.

Camera & vision

Use cameras/night vision for tells (duplicates, passengers, vanishing in NV). Confirm before acting to avoid false rejects.

Anomalies

Anomalies fall into four categories: Dangerous (reject), Safe (serve), Special (follow unique rules), and Environmental (acknowledge/wait). Some events are harmless scares unless you respond incorrectly.

Dangerous — Reject

  • Back Holes: Visible holes or dots on a customer’s back. Reject.
  • Nightvision Missing: Customer disappears in night vision. Reject.
  • CCTV Double: A duplicate appears on camera. Reject.
  • CCTV Passenger: Goblin-like rider attached to a customer’s back. Reject.
  • CCTV Screamer: Sudden lunge on camera. Reject.
  • Red Light, Green Light Spirit: Close shutters, reopen to pull him closer, he rushes forward; do not serve.
  • Santa (Merry Evening): Distorted Santa variant. Reject.
  • The Twins: Two blond women; they jerk unnaturally. Reject.
  • The Hacker: Threatens to crash/disconnect. Reject.

Safe — Serve

  • Karate Man: Martial arts outfit, practicing moves. Serve.
  • Dancing Lady: Dances in person (not just on camera). Serve.
  • Short Arms: Unusual proportions but harmless. Serve.
  • Phone Lady: Loud ringtone or screaming phone. Serve.
  • Drunk Guy: Intoxicated behavior; follow normal serving/payment rules. Serve if valid.

Special — Unique rules

  • Smiling Man: Serve a soda, take his slip and put under the desk.
  • Faceless Customer: Serve normally; do not hover the cursor over the face hole.
  • Disturbing Old Man in Fog: Seen far away; let him reach the kiosk, then close shutters to reject.
  • Booger Man: Decline his “offer” and continue working; do not engage.

Environmental — Acknowledge/Wait

  • Fog: Harmless on its own; treat normally unless paired with a specific event.
  • Dancing Goblin: Visual scare near kiosk; acknowledge only.
  • Fridge Goblin: Goblin near fridge; acknowledge only.
  • Window Watcher: Goblin staring through the window; no action needed.
  • The Meat: Turn the grill off immediately and leave the kiosk as directed.

Endings

Endings depend on your error count, inspection outcomes, and how you handle specific anomalies. Some are unlocked by following special steps (e.g., Smiling Man). Names and triggers may change with updates. If you are playing endless mode it is, obviously, endless and does not have any ending.

Good Ending

Low errors, correct handling of anomalies, and successful completion of scripted events without inspection failure.

Bad Ending

High error count, failed inspection, or critical mishandling of dangerous anomalies.

Secret/Variant Endings

Triggered via unique interactions (e.g., Smiling Man sequence). Community discoveries evolve over time.

Neutral Ending

Survive the shift with notable mistakes; pass but without optimal outcome.

Game Over

Immediate failure from lethal anomaly, reaching an error count of 5 or scripted death event.

Tips

Confirm before acting

Use cameras and night vision to verify tells (duplicate, passenger, vanishing) to avoid false rejects.

Reject = shutters

Close the shutters to reject. Some events require closing and reopening as part of the script.

Friday inspection

At 2:00 AM on Fridays the inspector visits. You must immediately exit the kiosk. Even a single glance can cause failure.

Timing matters

For the disturbing old man in fog, wait until he reaches the kiosk before rejecting. Closing early is counted as a mistake.

Stay calm in scares

Scripted scares are harmless if handled correctly. Don’t panic-serve or panic-reject.
